FACULTY OPENING
Coordinator, Tibetan Studies Information Community

The University of Virginia Library seeks a creative and energetic professional to serve as Coordinator, Tibetan Studies Information Community. This position will be responsible for forming, leading and marketing the Tibetan Studies Information Community to the University community and integrating it with the Tibetan digital library initiative based at the University of Virginia.

Environment: The University Library system consists of eleven libraries with independent libraries for health sciences, law, and business. The libraries serve 12,000 undergraduate and 6,000 graduate students and 1,000 teaching faculty. The Library's homepage can be accessed at http://www.lib.virginia.edu . Thomas Jefferson's Academical Village and the historic Grounds provide an inspiring background for teaching, learning and research.

Responsibilities: The Coordinator of the Tibetan Studies Information Community is responsible for the management and organization of the Tibetan Studies Information Community, reporting to the Director for Digital Services Initiatives. The Coordinator will work with other information community coordinators, various library members, and academic faculty across many disciplines to formulate priorities for the development of content, services and functionality. The Coordinator will assume responsibility for the creation and implementation of plans to address these priorities, and will maintain all private and public documentation of the Community activities, including the Tibetan Studies Information Community web site.

The Coordinator will work to disseminate information about the Community to a broader international community comprised of scholars, political and cultural persons and organizations, and the interested public, with the ultimate goal of engaging members of this broader community in the production and consumption of knowledge of Tibetan culture.

The Coordinator serves on a team of information community coordinators headed by the Director of Digital Services Initiatives that is responsible for developing a model information community infrastructure including policies and procedures needed to build and sustain information communities. The Coordinator will develop and implement a plan for integrating model information communities with traditional libraries and participate in national and international working groups of importance to traditional and digital libraries. The position will involve periodic international travel.

Qualifications: Masters degree in a relevant field, required. Knowledge and understanding of the use of digital information technologies within the context of intellectual endeavors, teaching and research, required; knowledge of spoken and literary Tibetan language and an understanding of the contemporary issues and scholarly study as it pertains to Tibet, required; a general understanding of and commitment to library standards and practices, required; strong interpersonal, leadership, and organizational skills are required as is the ability to manage multiple complex projects, required; excellent oral and written communication skills and a strong customer service orientation, required. Must be willing to travel internationally.

Salary and Benefits: Salary competitive depending on qualifications. General faculty status. The University of Virginia offers excellent benefits, including 22 days of vacation and TIAA/CREF and other optional retirement plans.

Review of applications will begin immediately and will continue until the position is filled. Applications received by October 31, 2001 will receive priority review. Send letter of application, resume, and names, addresses (including e-mail) and phone numbers of three references to: Mr. Alan R. Napier (arn3c@virginia.edu), Director of Library Human Resources and Planning, Alderman Library, PO Box 400114, University of Virginia, Charlottesville, VA 22904-4114.

The University and the Libraries have a strong commitment to achieving diversity among faculty and staff. The University of Virginia is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action employer. Minorities are encouraged to apply.
