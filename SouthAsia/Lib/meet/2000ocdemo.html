Part II: Various South Asia related sites or sites with SA content

A. Rebecca Moore: DSAL: Digital South Asia Library http://dsal.uchicago.edu/

B. David Magier:
Country information
   * EIU a) Country Data and b) ViewsWire http://www.eiu.com - most expensive service in this area
   * CountryWatch: http://www.countywatch.com - good for undergrads and basic business
   * Stratfor: http://www.stratfor.com/world/default.htm – mostly free, repackages from standard sources, good for quick overview
   * Euromoney Country Risk data: Each March and September, detailed risk data appears in the magazine; Euromoney is in Dow Jones Interactive; some older years available at euromoney.com archives

Jointly Administered Knowledge Environment (jake) -- http://jake.med.yale.edu/ -- used to identify where journals are indexed and/or available in full text

E-Books
   * University of California E-editions -- http://www.ucpress.edu/scan/books.html#3 -- free at this time; recently added Sarah Lamb's new book (White Saris and Sweet Mangoes)
   * NetLibrary: http://www.netlibrary.com - includes titles such as Many Ramayanas which are used repeatedly for Reserves; if your library purchases these, it is worth spending time to insure that Asian Studies titles are included

Update to Karl Potter's Bibliography (3rd ed.,1995) for Encyclopedia of Indian Philosophies - http://faculty.washington.edu/kpotter/ -- we've added to online catalog record for the latest edition;
Kluwer has Journal of Indian Philosophy and Indo-Iranian Journal available full text now
Indo-European Etymological Dictionary, including Lubotsky's Rgvedic Word Concordance -- http://iiasnt.leidenuniv.nl/ied/index.html

Art
   * AMICO - RLG/Eureka database if your library subscribes
   * Victoria & Albert Museum Images Online:
http://www.vam.ac.uk/Explorer/Virtual/images/ -- a dozen images from India, other museums have similar access to collections
TempleNet: http://www.indiantemples.com/ -- Indian temples including images, overview treatment

Poole's Plus: The Digital Index of the Nineteenth Century: http://www.poolesplus.net/ - includes books and several newspaper indexes; companion to PCI Web early coverage
Book Tables of Contents: Blackwell's Collection Manager or as database in RLG/Eureka -- can identify chapter level info if the MARC record has no notes or TOCs info included
